This graph was created using World Bank data.I downloaded all of the data and manipulated it in Excel in order to get the years of interest (from 2013 to 2017).I calculated the average of the % of government expenditure on education for each country. I observed that the highest expenditure is in Brazil (5.756%).Throughout the years, it can be observed from the graph that, Brazil is always above the other countries. Interactive bar chart-Percentage of secondary education attainment

This graph demonstrates another factor affecting inequality.This is because, when people attending secondary education have a higher possibility for higher wages than the rest of the people who do not attend secondary education resulting in higher inequality.

This interactive bar chart was made using World Bank data. The data has been manipulated in Excel, in order to include only the countries of interest.This factor is connected to the next graph, as I wanted to observe whether wages and secondary education are correlated. This was done because it is reasonable to believe that higher secondary education, leads to higher wages for those that attend this type of education, leading to higher inequality.

Regression between wages and secondary educational attainment

This graph is a regression between wages and secondary education in general in the World. The calculated R2= 0.02 suggests that, only 2% of the variation in the wages in the UK is explained by the % of secondary educational attainment. This further suggests that there are other factors that can affect wages and as a result affect income inequality. For example, gender wage gap and population growths.
