World Wide Inequality and some of its causes

Where is the highest inequality and how do we explain it?

The inequality in the world is measured by the Gini coefficient.
When the Gini coefficient is 0 it represents complete equality but when it is 100 it represents complete inequality.
This was done, by taking the data for worldwide Gini coefficient from World bank. I used a json file which included the coordinates for each country in the world.
In the Colab notebook, I matched the countries’ names with the countries’ names in the JSON file and matched their corresponding coordinates.

The highest income inequality is in the Namibia, with score of 59.1.
The project will mainly focus on India, Brazil, Argentina, China, as the top developing countries, and United Kingdom, USA, and Germany as the top developed countries.
Therefore, from the countries of interest, the greatest inequality is in Brazil (53.3) and USA with 41.5.

% of people attending secondary education-Chart3

Graph3, is the % of the population that attend secondary education.
This is another factor that affect inequality, because when people attend secondary education, it is reasonable to believe that they are going to receive higher wages than the rest of the people who do not
attend secondary education, resulting in higher inequality.
This graph was made by obtaining data from the World Bank for one specific year-2017. The biggest % is present in the United Kingdom, which can be a reasonable factor as the United Kingdom only has a Gini coefficient of 33.2, which is low in comparison to the rest of the countries discussed.
